Profile of Master Kim Ip:
In 1990, he started to learn martial arts. In 1992, he was inspired by Master Gray Lamand switched to playing Muay Thai. and Master Kim Ip's muay thai career also began. Since 1992, Master Kim Yip has set foot in the ring as a charming rookie and won the Hong Kong boxing championship in 1996. During this period, he accepted the challenges from countless foreign boxing champions and went to Thailand for training for more than two years. During his muay thai career, he fought in 30 professional competitions with a breathtaking results of 28 wins 2 loses. In addition, he has an unbeaten record in all amateur competitions. Then he went to Netherlands for special training in the later times of this muay thai career.

After winning three consecutive Hong Kong and international muay thai championships in 1998, in order to cultivate the next generation of muay thai fighters, Master Kim Ip announced his retirement from the ring. In the same year, he served as the head coach of Top-Fight Muay Thai Boxing Gym. He has taught many famous players. He has also taught the first batch of full-time professional muay thai coaches from Hong Kong who have long been staying in Thailand.

On November 11, 2004, he founded the Kim Ip Professional Muay Thai Fitness Center and the Kim Ip Production Studio. In the same year, he also established the first anti-drug Muay Thai competition and started advocating youngster to stay away from bad habits!

On December 10, 2005, the I-1 was established as the first international Muay Thai knockout tournament in Hong Kong. I-1 has been promoted to all parts of the world since 2005. It also receives many self-recommendations from foreign fighters every year. I-1 always selected fighters with a strict and serious attitude with the ultimate aim to determining the world champion in a competitive and fair environment. So as to flaunt the four "I": Invincibility, Intelligence, International and Intercontinental.

In 2006, the I-1 competition was promoted to 80 countries and became a famous brand in the international Muay Thai circle.

In 2007, the I-1 competition became the world's most famous and valuable international Muay Thai competition. I-1 attracted world-class Muay Thai fighters from all over the world so eager to participate in, and I-1 often received many letters of self-recommendation, videos and DVDs from world-class muay thai fighters.

In 2008, Master Kim Yip successfully held I-1 competitions in Europe and Thailand and evolved into a Muay Thai ambassador, and was constantly interviewed by TV shows and medias overseas.

In 2009 Master Kim Ip was invited by TVB as a guest of the "Hong Kong Power" tv program, encouraging Hong Kong people with his own experience! The I-1 event was broadcasted on TVB Pearl and HD channel. In the same year I-1 events are successfully held in The Grand Hyatt City of Dreams, Macau.

In 2010 he was awarded the Top Muay Thai Promoter by the World Muay Thai Council
In 2011 Master Yip accepted the invitation to be the President of the China Representative of the World Kickboxing Federation
In 2016 it was announced that I-1 became the oldest Muay Thai knockout tournament in the world and the only most vital and sustainable Muay Thai fighting tournament.
In 2016 successfully brought I-1 Muay Thai events to mainland China In 2016 Master Kim Ip is awarded the World Chinese Outstanding Youth. In October 2016, in the TV program "The Way of Health". He first sang publicly for charity and caring for the elders.

In 2017 Master Kim Ip is awarded the World Chinese Outstanding Youth of 2016. At the same time, he was written a biography as an outstanding person. This biography has been collected in many well-known universities and libraries in China, Hong Kong, Macao and Taiwan.
In April 2017 he vowed to build a temple for the God of Elephant in Hua Hin, so that the Buddha statue would not be graffitied by bird droppings, and the construction started in May.
On April 21, 2017 I-1 won the "Hong Kong's Most Popular Brand"
In June 2017 the I-1 WMC was classified as a China A-level event. Yuen Kai Chi, a famous and prestigious screenwriter in Hong Kong, wrote the film "Golden Robe Champion" with inspirations coming from Matser Kim Ip's true personal story.
